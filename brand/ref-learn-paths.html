Understanding Chain-of-Thought Prompting
Mission Guided
Build a Research Assistant Prompt
Create a chain-of-thought prompt that turns ChatGPT into a research assistant. Your prompt should guide the AI to break down complex questions into sub-questions, research each systematically, and synthesize findings.
  • Open ChatGPT or Claude in a new tab
  • Paste the starter prompt below and run it with a research question of your choice
  • Iterate on the prompt at least twice to improve the quality of the output
    • You are a research assistant. When I give you a question:

1. Break it into 3-5 sub-questions
2. Answer each sub-question with evidence
3. Synthesize into a final answer
4. Rate your confidence (low/medium/high)

Question: [your question here]
    Success Criteria
    • Prompt produces structured, multi-step output
    • At least 2 iterations show measurable improvement
    • Final output is usable without heavy editing
    ✓ Nailed it
    Your prompt demonstrates strong chain-of-thought structure. The breakdown into sub-questions is clear, and the confidence rating adds genuine value. The iterations show meaningful improvement in specificity.
    What's the primary benefit of chain-of-thought prompting over direct prompting?
    • It produces shorter responses
    • It makes the AI's reasoning process visible and debuggable
    • It uses fewer tokens
    • It works only with GPT-4
    How might you adapt this chain-of-thought technique for your specific work? Think about a real task you do weekly that involves research or analysis.
